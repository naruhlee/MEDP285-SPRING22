Argentina - 1900s


Tango originated in Argentina, its root comes from the working class people.
Tango first spread through Europe. It was the music of European Immigrants, former slaves, and the working class

The accordion was brought to Latin America through German Migrants. It quickly spread to the United States
after arrivng to Argentina.

Pictured above is Astor Piazzolla playing the accordion.
In the song, you can hear the accordion being one of the strongest instruments.

Cuba - 1930s


Mambo originted in Cuba around the 1930s. It is the mixture of Afro-Carribean and Latin American Culture
Evolved in Havana on Haitian settlements in Cuba.
Charangas, a band that played traditional Cuban dance music helped evolve the Mambo style. The word "Mambo" means voodoo priestess.

New York City - 1960's


In the late '60s, U.S.- based Afro Caribbean musicians fused dance structures from Cuba (such as Mambo) with a Puerto Rican taste.
They also included influences of American jazz, funk, and R&B. From this, Salsa was born!!
uerto Ricans had migrated during this time through the BRACEROS program which offered jobs for Mexican/Puerto Rican Immigrants in America.
Which is why the origin of Salsa begins in New York.

Latin America - 1970


Heading into the '70s, the influence of the Beatles, the Rolling Stones and Bob Dylan motivated a generation of young Latinos to pick up electric guitars and compose rock anthems.
El Chicano formed in Los Angeles. Later came Argentina's Soda Stereo and Mexico's Caifanes.

Dominican Republic - 1970's & 90's


With the arrival of Dominican immigrants in New York City,
There was a fusion of merengue and bachata.
Juan Luis Guerra was the first artist to infuse both poetry and verve.
Aventura is a band that originated in the Bronx with Dominican band members.
This is an example of how Bachata entered the U.S.A. with the help of younger generations.

Texas 1950s - 1990s


After the Mexican Revolution, European migrants arrived in Texas. With them, they brought the accordion, polka and waltzes
These sounds fused together naturally with the influence of Mariachi, Corridos, Cumbia, Jazz and some Rock n' Roll
Lydia Mendoza (pictured on the right) was born in Houston, and she was the mother of Tejano Music.
In her music, you hear the beginnings of this music genre, very natural and played simply on a guitar.
Soon after her, came the legendary Selena!Selena incorporated Cumbia, Jazz, and sometimes Pop in her music.

You can hear the evolution of this genre and the incorporation of different instruments.
In the 90s, Selena became the face of Tejano Music until the end of her life at the age of 23.

U.S.A. & LATAM 1990s


In the 2000s there was a generation of bilingual pop stars.
They brought Latin Music into America after years of influence,
they took every genre before this yearand created a hybrid of pan-Latin rhythms with a pop aesthetic.
Pop artists such as Shakira, Juanes, and Ricky Martin sang songs in both Spanish and English.
They collaborated with English artists as well.
For example, the song “Fotografía” (photograph) sung by Juanaes features Nelly Furtado.
Nelly Furtado was an English artist who dominantly sang in English and produced her
popular song "I'm like a bird," two years before recording with Juanes.

Panama - 1970s


Some people don't know that Reggaeton originated in Panama. It began as reggae.
El General was an artist in Panama who set the foundation of reggae which later becomes reggaeton.
Puerto Rican artists such as Ivy Queen, Tego Calderon & Don Omar built upon the foundation.
They added their tropical influences and American rap — and from this, Reggaeton was born.
Now we see Reggaetón as one of the most popular genres among Latinos, specifically younger audiences
Recently we have seen Reggaeton collaborate with other genres as well!
For example, Aventura, the bachata band of the 90s has made a comeback recently
and collaborated with Reggaeton artist Bad Bunny to fuse both genres.
